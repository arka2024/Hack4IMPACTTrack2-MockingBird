THE THREE BRUTAL FACTS

FACT 01

They knew and didn't fix it.

Tesla's spokesperson said the adversarial attack was unrealistic "given that a driver can easily override Autopilot at any time" — which completely misses the point that the driver may not even know an attack is happening until the swerve occurs.

SOURCE: MIT TECHNOLOGY REVIEW
FACT 02

Older cars are permanently exposed.

The tape attack only affects older Tesla vehicles carrying the MobilEye EyeQ3 camera system. Those cars cannot be hardware-upgraded — millions of them are still on roads today with no fix possible through conventional means.

SOURCE: THE REGISTER
FACT 03

Cross-sensor validation is not enough.

Radar tells Tesla there's nothing physically blocking the road. The camera says "speed limit is 85." These don't contradict each other — so the validation passes and the car accelerates anyway. Cross-sensor validation only helps when the sensors disagree. Adversarial sign attacks don't create disagreement — they fool just the camera, which radar cannot verify independently.
